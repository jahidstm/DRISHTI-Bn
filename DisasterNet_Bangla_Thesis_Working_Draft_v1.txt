3.3 Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning (PEFT)


Modifying every weight within the integrated Vision Transformer and BanglaBERT networks presents an insurmountable computational barrier [REF]. Standard adaptation protocols mandate comprehensive gradient calculations across billions of independent variables. Hardware capacities exhaust rapidly. Updating all parameters simultaneously requires massive video random access memory, which is completely unavailable in standard regional environments [REF]. Executing full architectural retraining remains impossible under these strict physical constraints.


Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA) resolves this structural obstacle entirely [REF]. The algorithm bypasses exhaustive parameter updates by permanently locking the pre-existing dense layer components. Specifically, the original pre-trained model weights W0 receive no gradient alterations during the training phase. The framework simultaneously injects two adjustable rank decomposition matrices, denoted mathematically as A and B, directly parallel to these frozen layers. The strategy succeeds.


This specific mathematical intervention restructures the internal data flow. The modified forward pass incorporates the injected matrices to compute the localized feature update. The exact mathematical execution follows the formula W0 + BA. Within this equation, the rank parameter r=8 dictates the inner dimension of the multiplicative matrices. Researchers deliberately configure r=8 to remain exceptionally tiny compared to the expansive original matrix dimensions. This precise mathematical factorization shrinks the trainable parameter count by orders of magnitude [REF].


Such algorithmic economy drives the entire proposed system. DisasterNet-Bangla depends entirely upon this specialized mathematical reduction. The massive multimodal framework successfully executes simultaneous flood severity categorization and Bengali text generation without exceeding local processing constraints. Deploying sophisticated emergency artificial intelligence in real-world, hardware-limited environments becomes fully viable.
